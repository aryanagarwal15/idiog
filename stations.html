Bargad

Bargad, the soft power think tank of Idiog has been created with the mission of promoting the culture and heritage of India. Bargad works on the vision of inclusivity and aims to empower people and thus also ensures that the authenticity of the culture and traditions is not compromised on. It will primarily be responsible for providing professional expertise and enabling the implementation of the various means by which the promotion of the culture and heritage of the Konkan region will be achieved.

Urbania

  • Micro-finance and the Business Correspondent model
  • Sexual Health Clinic for Women
  • Gender Action Research Fellowship
  • Collaboration with Anant National Fellowship
  • Station

The RBI had rightly promoted the BC model to ensure that banking services are available to economically as well as geographically disadvantaged sections of the society in order to ensure financial inclusion. After a decade of implementation of BC model, a study is required on the success of BC model in the urban slum area and what other savings opportunities are currently accessed by the bottom of pyramid population.

Women in slums or those coming from marginalised sections of the society find it difficult to access health product that are closely associated with their sexual/reproductive well-being. The attempt in this case will be to set up health clinics with which are run by women to tackle the issue of shame or apprehension faced by potential female customers while demanding such services. This will be tested in Mumbai on pilot basis.

The proposed fellowship model will be implemented in Mumbai in collaboration with MahaSuvidha (Maharashtra Suvidha Nirman Sanstha) in Mumbai for 50 BMC schools. The proposed Gender Education Research Fellowship Programme will provide an opportunity to young education enthusiasts who want to make primary education more inclusive and sensitive towards female students. The research fellowship aims to facilitate a world class learning environment and conduct a policy research over the period of one year. Fellows have to play a dual role, first as an education facilitator and second as a policy researcher based on a yearlong field interaction.

Urbania will act as a facilitator for community outreach for the Anant National Fellowship. The fellowship is based on the theme of encouraging sustainable and equitable solutions to the built environment in India. Urbania will connect the fellows with the community that is an essential component of this structure ranging from ULB's to CBO's. Urbania will also be actively engaged in connecting the students with the mentors and efficient use of media to assess the impact of the projects designed by the fellows.

In Indian setup, when we say station that means either railway station or bus station, which simply implies to crowded places that enable peoples’ mobility in all direction regardless of their social or political identity. Similarly, Station brings a platform for discussing the issues that India needs to discuss in the 21st century. The annual summit tries to talk about the issues ranging from cosmopolitan states, capital cities, 3 tier cities to villages.

Pragya

The ongoing Pragya Balika Fellowship will provide an opportunity to young education enthusiasts who want to make primary education more inclusive and sensitive towards female students. The duration of the program will run through an academic session (April 2018 to April 2019); the application process will start from the month of January 2018. The gender research fellowship aims to facilitate an inclusive learning with tangible outcomes in terms of policy briefs and models that reflect and ensure gender friendly school environment, learning outcome and primary education gendered policy intervention over the period of one year.
